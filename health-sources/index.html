Health information sources

OxynX’s preventive, nutrition, activity, and general health guidance is informed by trusted, publicly available, and widely recognized health authorities. Below are the primary sources our content draws from, with links you can review directly.

OxynX organizes information and surfaces general, educational health and preventive guidance. That guidance is based on the publicly available recommendations of the organizations listed below. OxynX does not provide a diagnosis and is not a substitute for professional medical advice.

Prevention & screenings

U.S. Preventive Services Task Force (USPSTF)
Evidence-based recommendations for preventive screenings and services.
uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org
Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
U.S. public-health guidance on prevention, vaccination, and conditions.
cdc.gov

General health information

MedlinePlus — U.S. National Library of Medicine (NIH)
Plain-language health and condition information from the NIH.
medlineplus.gov
World Health Organization (WHO)
International public-health guidance and fact sheets.
who.int

Nutrition

Dietary Guidelines for Americans (USDA & HHS)
Federal nutrition guidance underpinning meal and calorie recommendations.
dietaryguidelines.gov
MyPlate (USDA)
Practical, personalized nutrition and portion guidance.
myplate.gov

Physical activity & workouts

Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans (HHS)
Federal guidance on exercise type, frequency, and intensity.
health.gov

Medications

U.S. Food & Drug Administration (FDA)
Authoritative drug labeling, safety, and approval information.
fda.gov/drugs

Reproductive & cycle health

American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG)
Clinical guidance for menstrual, cycle, and reproductive health.
acog.org
Office on Women’s Health (OASH, HHS)
Government guidance on women’s and reproductive health.
womenshealth.gov

OxynX is not a medical provider and does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. The information in the app is general and educational, drawn from the sources above, and is not a substitute for the judgment of a qualified health professional.
